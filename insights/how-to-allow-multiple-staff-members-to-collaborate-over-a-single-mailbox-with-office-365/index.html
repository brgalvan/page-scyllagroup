How to Allow Multiple Staff Members to Collaborate Over a Single Mailbox with Office 365

by Morgan Little
October 2021

<

Often we have clients who leverage resources or Shared Mailboxes in their organization which allows multiple staff members to collaborate over a single mailbox.

Even better, with Office 365, Shared Mailboxes do not require additional licensing to be assigned to them making it an affordable solution. Good examples of when a shared mailbox is used are a jobs@ email address where multiple staff members need to review job applicant information or reception@ where the staff supporting the front reception. In those situations, it can sometimes get overwhelming trying to maintain your email in Outlook as well as monitoring and accessing the Shared Mailbox. With that being said, we wanted to provide the steps for some tools and options you can leverage to better work in a Shared Mailbox. The first step is creating or gaining access to your Shared Mailbox.

Accessing Shared Mailbox in Outlook

After the Shared Mailbox is created and your access established, you should be the mailbox listed in the left pane in Outlook. You can select the arrow next to the name to expand the mailbox and see the Inbox, Sent, etc (example below).

Example of Shared Mailbox in Outlook

 

After expanding the Shared Mailbox, you may notice that it may be difficult to see the folders of your mailbox as well as the folders in of the Shared Mailbox. This is when we recommend that you use the Outlook favorites options. For example, say you need to monitor the Inbox of your new Reception Shared Mailbox while still working within your primary email. You can add a shortcut to your Outlook favorites by single right-clicking on the Reception Inbox folder and choosing “Add to Favorites”. After it is added you should see it listed in the Favorites at the top of your left pane in Outlook. This is a very nice option since you’ll see a bold number listed in the Inbox when an unread message is there.

Example of Favorites in Outlook

Accessing Shared Mailbox through Outlook Webmail

Just in case you do not have access to Outlook or you requested that the Shared Mailbox not be linked (great option for better performance in Outlook), you can also leverage the Webmail to Open Another Mailbox. Here are those steps:

  1. Sign in to your account through Outlook WebMail - https://outlook.office365.com
    NOTE: Make sure to log in with your primary account information.
  2. On the Outlook Web App navigation bar, select your name in the top right. In the new menu, choose “Open Another Mailbox”.

3. In the new window, type in the email address of the Shared Mailbox then select Open. The mailbox will open in a new tab on your browser. 

Sending Email from a Different Email Address

When responding to an email from a Shared Mailbox, Outlook is intelligent enough to automatically list the response email as being from the Shared Mailbox instead of your primary email address. What if you need to start a new message from the Shared Mailbox? Turning on the “From” option on new messages will allow you to add the email address. Even better, Outlook keeps this address cached so after you do it once, it will be an option for you to change easily down the road. Here are the steps on how you can accomplish this.  

  1. Open a new email in Outlook
  2. In the new message window, choose the Options Tab and select the “From” in the Show Fields section to have the From option appears in the new message.
  3. Select the From Button to see a list of options and choose “Other Email Address”.
  4. In the Send From Other Email Address Dialog Window, type in or use From to find the Shared Mailbox email address.
    NOTE: If the mailbox was just configured, you may need to wait 1 day for it to appear in the address book.
  5. After the address is added, you’ll notice that the email address in the new message was changed. Since you added this address once to your From List, Outlook will remember the email address and always be available to select.

Additional Advice on Shared Mailboxes

  Here are a couple of additional options you can use with Shared Mailboxes:

  1. Since Shared Mailboxes are often accessed by multiple staff members, it is important to remember that if someone reads a message it is marked as read so all other staff members see it as a read message. If someone accidentally opened an email, they could always single right-click the message and change it to Unread.
  2. Shared Mailboxes are not only Emails but include all Mailbox resources like Calendar and Contacts.
  3. Wanting to access Shared Mailboxes from your mobile devices? This can easily be done through the latest versions of Outlook for iOS and Outlook for Android apps installed on Mobile Devices.
    Microsoft Outlook for iOS and Android | Microsoft 365
  4. If you find you are responding or creating emails from the Shared Mailbox, you may want to create an email signature so you can add it to your email messages.
    Create and add a signature to messages (microsoft.com)
  5.  Let us know if you would like the emails you send or delete from the Shared Mailbox to remain in the Shared Mailbox. Out of the box, Outlook does not do this but we can easily toggle this option on if you would like it.
    Messages sent from a shared mailbox aren't saved to the Sent Items folder - Exchange | Microsoft Docs

We understand many of you have encountered unique work environments since the pandemic and we hope that these tips and tricks help no matter where you are located.
